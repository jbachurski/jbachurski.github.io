Fabric

Jakub Bachurski
Supervisors: Dominic Orchard and Alan Mycroft
University of Cambridge

Abstract

Subtyping is a classical programming language feature, usually associated with nominal subtyping in object-oriented programming. Structural subtyping — where the subtyping relation is derived from the shared structure of types — is an alternative. Various structural typing approaches have seen interest, but these are often incomplete without subtyping, which ends up omitted because of its complexity.
I argue that with the introduction of algebraic subtyping — a new framework for ML-style type inference — the time has come to revisit structural subtyping. In my thesis I propose a novel language design, Fabric, and demonstrate the relevance of structural subtyping to modern design problems. As part of the project, I propose a new approach to typing array programs, show how to capture record extension within algebraic subtyping, and compile Fabric into WebAssembly with a focus on efficient core data structures. I aim to show structural subtyping could narrow the expressive gap between static and dynamic languages, and its ideas can improve how we use both.
